Heart System · Patterns

Heart Blood Stasis

Blood Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Circulatory/Nutritive (Blood Axis): Obstructed coronary perfusion from atherosclerotic and thrombogenic processes.

Pathophysiology

Coronary atherosclerotic plaque with reduced myocardial perfusion, endothelial dysfunction, and platelet hyperaggregability.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Elevated LDL oxidation, elevated inflammatory markers within atherosclerotic plaque (IL-6, hs-CRP), elevated platelet activation markers (thromboxane A2).

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Fixed, stabbing chest pain that may radiate to the shoulder or arm, purplish lips and tongue, palpitations, and a sensation of oppression in the chest.

Tongue

Dark purple or dusky body, possibly with visible stasis spots.

Pulse

Choppy (Se) or Knotted/Intermittent (Jie/Dai), rough and uneven under the fingers.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Episodic or chronic, often triggered or worsened by exertion, cold, or emotional stress. Fixed in location, worse at night.
> rest, warmth, gradual activity
< exertion, cold, emotional stress, fatty food

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Fixed stabbing chest pain radiating to the left shoulder or arm, occasional jaw or upper back referred discomfort.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Suppressed anxiety about the heart itself, a guarded quality around exertion, occasionally a stoic minimization of symptoms that delays seeking care.

Biomedical Correlates

Coronary artery disease, angina pectoris, myocardial infarction, atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease generally.

Confirming Lab Markers

Cardiac: Elevated troponin if acute infarction; coronary angiography or CT angiography showing stenotic lesions; elevated LDL and Lp(a).

Inflammatory: Elevated hs-CRP as an independent cardiovascular risk marker.
