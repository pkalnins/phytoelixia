Heart System · Patterns

Heart Yang Deficiency

Yang Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Reduced cardiac contractile and metabolic output, with peripheral hypoperfusion.

Pathophysiology

Reduced cardiac contractility and output, reduced peripheral perfusion, and — in severe presentations — inadequate autonomic compensatory reserve (Heart Yang Collapse, correlating with cardiogenic shock).

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Reduced β-adrenergic receptor sensitivity, reduced myocardial ATP production (mitochondrial dysfunction), elevated but ultimately insufficient BNP/NT-proBNP compensatory signaling.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Palpitations, cold extremities, chest oppression or pain worse with cold, pale or purplish lips, spontaneous sweating, and fatigue; in severe cases, cold sweat with a barely perceptible pulse (Heart Yang Collapse).

Tongue

Pale body, possibly with a purplish tinge, moist coating.

Pulse

Deep (Chen) and Weak (Ruo), occasionally Knotted or Intermittent (Jie/Dai) reflecting irregular cardiac rhythm.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Chronic, insidious onset, markedly worse in cold weather and with exertion. Worse in winter.
> warmth, rest, gentle movement
< cold, overexertion, sudden exertion after rest

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Cold extremities, generalized fatigue-related weakness, chest oppression worse with cold or exertion.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Low drive and a diminished sense of warmth or connection, mild apathy, a "banked low" quality mirroring the reduced cardiac output beneath it.

Biomedical Correlates

Congestive heart failure with reduced ejection fraction, bradyarrhythmias, cardiogenic shock (severe presentation), hypothyroidism with cardiac involvement (myxedema heart).

Confirming Lab Markers

Cardiac: Elevated BNP/NT-proBNP; echocardiogram showing reduced ejection fraction.

Endocrine: TSH elevated with free T3/T4 low-normal or low if hypothyroid.

ECG: Bradycardia or conduction abnormalities where relevant.
