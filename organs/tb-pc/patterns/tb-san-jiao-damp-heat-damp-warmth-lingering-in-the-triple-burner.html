Triple Burner-Pericardium System · Patterns

TB: San Jiao Damp-Heat (Damp-Warmth Lingering in the Triple Burner)

Yin Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Fluid (Yin Axis): Persistent, low-grade systemic inflammatory/damp burden spanning all three Burners — the classic Damp-Warmth (湿温) presentation of Warm Disease theory.

Pathophysiology

Gut-associated systemic low-grade inflammation, dysbiosis-driven endotoxemia, and sluggish lymphatic clearance producing a persistent, hard-to-resolve low-grade inflammatory state.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Chronically elevated but modest IL-6/CRP, elevated LPS-binding protein reflecting gut-derived endotoxemia.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Low-grade persistent fever, a heavy sensation throughout the body, chest and epigastric fullness, poor appetite — notoriously slow to resolve compared to acute heat patterns.

Tongue

Greasy, thick, yellow-white coating covering the whole surface.

Pulse

Soggy (Ru) or Slippery (Hua), soft and rolling rather than sharply forceful.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Gradual onset, protracted course, worse in humid weather and summer. Notoriously slow to resolve once established.
> dry climate, light diet, patience with gradual resolution
< humidity, greasy/fatty food, alcohol

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Generalized heaviness and achiness, joint stiffness with a damp quality, lethargy disproportionate to any single finding.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Mental fog and a "stuck," unresolving quality to mood — low-grade malaise that doesn't clear even with rest, mirroring the pattern's own slow-to-resolve nature.

Biomedical Correlates

Prolonged or atypical infections with persistent low-grade fever, metabolic syndrome with systemic low-grade inflammation, post-viral fatigue syndromes with a prominent GI/inflammatory component.

Confirming Lab Markers

Inflammatory: Modestly elevated CRP/ESR, persisting longer than expected for the apparent severity of illness.

GI: Elevated LPS-binding protein or zonulin if gut permeability is being assessed; stool studies showing dysbiotic pattern.

Herb Classes

Transform damp, clear heat, aromatic transformation
