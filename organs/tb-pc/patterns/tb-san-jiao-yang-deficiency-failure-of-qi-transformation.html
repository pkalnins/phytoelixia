Triple Burner-Pericardium System · Patterns

TB: San Jiao Yang Deficiency (Failure of Qi Transformation)

Yang Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Hypofunction of the whole fluid-metabolism pathway — the transformation and movement of fluid across all three Burners running cold and sluggish.

Pathophysiology

Reduced aldosterone/ADH regulation combined with reduced cardiac and renal output, and reduced capillary oncotic pressure, together producing generalized rather than localized fluid retention.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Reduced RAAS-appropriate compensatory response, reduced hepatic albumin synthesis, reduced renal sodium/water handling.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Generalized edema (face, limbs, and in advanced cases the whole body), reduced urination, cold extremities, fatigue, poor appetite, loose stools.

Tongue

Pale, swollen body with a wet white coating.

Pulse

Deep (Chen) and Weak (Ruo) throughout all three positions.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Chronic, insidious onset, worse in cold weather and toward the end of the day (dependent edema). Worse with prolonged standing/sitting.
> warmth, rest, elevation of swollen limbs
< cold, prolonged standing, excess salt/fluid intake

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Generalized heaviness in the limbs, cold extremities, mild joint stiffness from tissue edema.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Low drive and a diminished, waterlogged mental quality — heaviness in thought mirroring heaviness in the body.

Biomedical Correlates

Generalized/systemic edema states (nephrotic syndrome, right heart failure with anasarca, hypoalbuminemia), hypothyroid-associated myxedema.

Confirming Lab Markers

Renal/Hepatic: Low serum albumin; proteinuria if nephrotic; elevated creatinine if renal function is impaired.

Cardiac: Elevated BNP/NT-proBNP if right heart failure is contributing.

Endocrine: TSH elevated with free T3/T4 low-normal or low if hypothyroid.

Herb Classes

Warm and promote Qi transformation, disinhibit water

  • Cinnamon bark (Rou gui)
  • Prepared aconite (Fu zi)
  • Poria (Fu ling)
  • Polyporus (Zhu ling)
