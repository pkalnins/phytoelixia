Triple Burner-Pericardium System · Patterns

Pc: Heat Entering the Pericardium

Yang Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Acute systemic inflammatory crisis with blood-brain barrier disruption — pathogenic heat breaching the Pericardium's protective role en route to the Heart.

Pathophysiology

Systemic inflammatory response with blood-brain barrier disruption, cytokine-mediated encephalopathy, and hyperthermia-associated altered consciousness.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Markedly elevated systemic IL-6/TNF-α, disrupted blood-brain barrier tight-junction protein expression, elevated central inflammatory markers where CSF is sampled.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

High fever, delirium or confused speech, restlessness progressing to loss of consciousness in severe cases — the classic Warm Disease crisis pattern.

Tongue

Dark red body, especially the tip, may appear dry.

Pulse

Rapid (Shuo) and forceful, may become faint or hidden as consciousness declines in the most severe presentations.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Acute, rapidly progressive, a medical emergency. Worse as fever climbs.
> immediate medical treatment, cooling, rest
< delayed treatment, dehydration, further heat exposure

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Generalized rigidity or restlessness depending on the stage, possible neck stiffness if meningeal involvement is present.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Confusion progressing to delirium and, in the most severe cases, loss of consciousness — the Shen overwhelmed as pathogenic heat breaches its protective barrier.

Biomedical Correlates

Sepsis with encephalopathy, severe bacterial meningitis or encephalitis, high fever-associated delirium in children or the critically ill.

Confirming Lab Markers

This is a medical emergency requiring immediate biomedical evaluation, not herbal management alone.

Inflammatory/Infectious: Markedly elevated CRP/procalcitonin, blood and CSF cultures where indicated, CSF analysis for meningitis/encephalitis.
