Triple Burner-Pericardium System · Patterns

Pc: Phlegm-Heat Misting the Pericardium

Yin Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Fluid (Yin Axis): Acute infection-driven fluid/secretory burden compromising consciousness — distinguished from Heart's own chronic Phlegm Misting pattern by its acute, febrile-illness-specific onset.

Pathophysiology

Acute systemic infection with secondary CNS involvement and excessive airway or oral secretions, typically arising in the context of critical illness rather than a chronic structural or metabolic process.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Elevated CSF/serum inflammatory markers in infectious encephalopathy, elevated mucin production in concurrent respiratory infection.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Stupor or coma with a gurgling sound of phlegm in the throat, high fever, possible seizure activity — an acute, infection-driven presentation.

Tongue

Thick, greasy, yellow coating; body may be dark.

Pulse

Slippery (Hua) and Rapid (Shuo), rolling quickly.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Acute, arising in the context of severe febrile or infectious illness. A medical emergency.
> immediate medical treatment, airway support, rest
< delayed treatment, aspiration risk if secretions are unmanaged

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Generalized flaccidity or rigidity depending on the stage of illness, minimal spontaneous movement in severe stupor.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Clouded consciousness progressing to stupor — the Shen's clarity obscured by both heat and accumulated Phlegm simultaneously, a more severe and acute picture than Heart's own chronic Phlegm-Misting pattern.

Biomedical Correlates

Sepsis-associated encephalopathy, severe pneumonia with delirium, acute bacterial meningitis with copious respiratory secretions.

Confirming Lab Markers

This is a medical emergency requiring immediate biomedical evaluation, not herbal management alone.

Infectious/Neurological: Blood and CSF cultures where indicated, neuroimaging to rule out structural cause, EEG if seizure activity is present.
