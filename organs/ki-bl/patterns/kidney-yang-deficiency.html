Kidney-Bladder System · Patterns

Kidney Yang Deficiency

Yang Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Hypometabolic, hypoendocrine activity across the HPA/HPG/thyroid axes and renal concentrating function — the Root itself running cold.

Pathophysiology

Reduced HPA/HPG axis output, reduced renal concentrating ability, reduced mitochondrial thermogenesis, and blunted aldosterone/ADH regulation.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Reduced steroidogenic enzyme activity (gonadal and adrenal), reduced UCP-mediated thermogenesis, reduced renal aquaporin-2 expression (concentrating defect), blunted thyroid receptor sensitivity.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Cold limbs (especially the feet), low back and knee weakness or pain worse with cold, impotence or low libido, infertility, frequent clear or copious urination, nocturia, dependent or facial edema.

Tongue

Pale, swollen body with a wet, white coating.

Pulse

Deep (Chen) and Weak (Ruo), especially faint at both Chi (Kidney) positions.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Chronic, insidious onset, markedly worse in cold weather and in the early morning. Worse in winter.
> warmth, rest, gentle movement
< cold, overexertion, sleep deprivation

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Low back and knee weakness worse with cold and improved by warmth, generalized heaviness in the limbs, cold extremities.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Low drive and diminished will-forces (Zhi, the Kidney's associated aspect of will/drive), apathy, reluctance to initiate — a deep constitutional "banked low" quality.

Biomedical Correlates

Hypothyroidism, adrenal insufficiency, hypogonadism, chronic kidney disease with reduced GFR, age-related nocturia, erectile dysfunction of vascular/hormonal origin.

Confirming Lab Markers

Endocrine: TSH elevated with free T3/T4 low-normal or low; low-normal or low testosterone/estradiol; low-normal morning cortisol.

Renal: Elevated creatinine/reduced eGFR if CKD is present; reduced urine osmolality reflecting a concentrating defect.
