Kidney-Bladder System · Patterns

Kidney Yin Deficiency

Yin Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Fluid (Yin Axis): Chronic structural fluid depletion across the renal and reproductive-hormonal systems, without overt heat-flaring.

Pathophysiology

Reduced renal concentrating capacity, mild chronic HPA-axis dysregulation, and declining gonadal hormone reserve, without the acute vasomotor flaring seen in the Deficient Fire variant.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Mild chronic elevation of FSH/LH with declining estradiol/testosterone, reduced renal aquaporin-2 expression, mild chronic elevation of inflammatory markers without acute-flare intensity.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Dizziness, tinnitus, low back and knee soreness and weakness, night sweats, five-center heat, dry mouth (especially at night), premature graying, mild memory difficulty.

Tongue

Red body with little or no coating.

Pulse

Thready (Xi) and Rapid (Shuo), fine and fast, weak at both Chi positions.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Chronic, insidious onset, worse in the afternoon and evening, worse in hot weather.
> rest, cooling/moistening foods, quiet evenings
< overwork, heat exposure, insufficient sleep

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Low back and knee soreness and weakness, mild nocturnal muscle cramping.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

A quieter, more chronic version of the restlessness seen in Deficient Fire — mild anxiety, occasional insomnia, a sense of running on a low reserve without acute agitation.

Biomedical Correlates

Early or mild chronic kidney disease, milder menopausal syndrome, osteoporosis with mild systemic heat signs, chronic mild dehydration or electrolyte imbalance.

Confirming Lab Markers

Renal: Mildly reduced eGFR or mildly elevated creatinine in early CKD; reduced urine specific gravity.

Endocrine: Mildly elevated FSH/LH with declining estradiol/testosterone; normal or mildly elevated evening cortisol.
