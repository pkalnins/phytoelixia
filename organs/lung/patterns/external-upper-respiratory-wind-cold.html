Lung-Respiratory System · Patterns

External · Upper Respiratory · Wind-Cold

Yang Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Acute hypometabolic vasoconstriction — a local deficiency of metabolic-immune (Yang) activity in the upper respiratory field, not an invading excess (Cold Deficiency, Upper focus).

Pathophysiology
  • Vasoconstriction
  • Localized hypothermia of the upper respiratory tract
  • Delayed early-phase viral clearance due to poor local perfusion
Gene / Signaling Pathways

Down-regulated eNOS (suppressed nitric oxide/vasodilation), delayed IFN−α/β expression, and initial up-regulation of cyclooxygenase-2 (COX−2).

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Acute cough with thin, clear, watery, or white frothy sputum. Pronounced aversion to cold, goosebumps, low-grade fever (where the chills clearly outweigh the fever), a total lack of sweating, sneezing, nasal congestion with clear watery discharge, and a dull, occipital headache radiating down the neck.

Tongue

Normal body color with a thin, white, and moist/wet coating.

Pulse

Floating (Fu) and Tight (Jin), feeling superficial, bouncy, and rigid like a stretched wire.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Sudden onset with cold exposure. Worse in cold/damp weather, mornings.
> warmth, rest, covering up
< wind, cold exposure, drafts

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Diffuse body aches, occipital headache radiating into neck/shoulders, stiffness with chills, muscle tension from shivering.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

A desire to withdraw, curl up under blankets, and avoid stimulation. Mental processes may feel temporarily sluggish or foggy, mirroring the constricting, slowing nature of pathogenic Cold.

Biomedical Correlates
  • The early incubation phase of the common cold (Rhinovirus, Coronavirus)
  • Acute viral rhinopharyngitis
  • Cold-induced asthma flares
Confirming Lab Markers
  • Complete Blood Count (CBC): Normal white blood cell (WBC) count or mild leukopenia with relative lymphocytosis, typical of an early viral process.
  • Multiplex PCR Respiratory Panel: Positive for common respiratory viruses (Rhinovirus, RSV) without bacterial markers.
Herb Classes
  • Warming diaphoretics
  • Pungent stimulants (these address lower respiratory tract Yang deficiency)
