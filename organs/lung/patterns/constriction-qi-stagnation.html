Lung-Respiratory System · Patterns

Constriction (Qi Stagnation)

Qi Excess
Primary Biological Axis
  • Autonomic (Qi Axis): SNS & Sensory-Motor Hyper-reactivity
  • Uncoordinated neurogenic tone.
Pathophysiology
  • Hyperexpresson of airway nociceptors (TRPV1, TRPA1)
  • Elevated Substance P/CGRP
  • Localized spasmodic bronchospasms
  • Receptor desensitization.
Gene / Signaling Pathways

TRPV1, TRPA1 (nociceptor sensitization); Substance P, CGRP (neurogenic inflammation); elevated circulating catecholamines; reduced heart-rate variability/vagal tone.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Sensation of constriction, tightness or a "blockage" in the chest (Plum Pit Qi), frequent sighing, spasmodic ticklish cough.

Tongue

Normal color, often a thin white coating; tip may appear slightly red or tremulous with anxiety.

Pulse

Wiry (Xian), tight, occasionally uneven with breath-holding; often faster when anxious.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Episodic, triggered by stress or emotion.
> movement, breathing exercises, emotional expression
< suppressed emotion, confined spaces, breath-holding

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Chest wall/intercostal tightness, diaphragmatic tension, upper trapezius and sternocleidomastoid (accessory breathing muscle) tension, tension headaches from breath-holding.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Anxiety, breath-holding, a subjective sense of being unable to get a full breath, hypervigilance toward bodily sensation, and a tendency toward catastrophizing that reinforces the somatic loop of panic and constriction.

Biomedical Correlates
  • Stress-induced bronchospasm
  • Hyperventilation syndrome
  • Asthma (cough-variant)
  • Globus pharyngeus
Confirming Lab Markers
  • Positive methacholine challenge test
  • Elevated circulating catecholamines or salivary cortisol
  • Abnormal Esophageal Manometry
