Lung-Respiratory System · Patterns

Phlegm-Damp

Yin Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Fluid (Yin Axis): Excess fluid accumulation and transport failure.

Pathophysiology
  • Hypersecretion of low-viscosity or gel-like mucins
  • Impaired ciliary beat frequency
  • Metabolic stagnation (often linked to Spleen/insulin resistance networks)
Gene / Signaling Pathways

Up-regulated MUC5AC and MUC5B mucin genes, down-regulated FOXA2 (which normally suppresses mucus production), and elevated metabolic leukotrienes.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Chronic cough with copious, white, thick, sticky or easy-to-expectorate sputum, chest oppression, systemic heaviness, mental fog. Pale, swollen tongue with thick, white, greasy coating; Slippery (Hua) pulse.

Tongue

Pale, swollen, or enlarged body with a thick, white, greasy, or sticky coating.

Pulse

Slippery (Hua) and Soggy/Soft (Ru), rolling smoothly under the fingers like pearls sliding through a basin.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Gradual, chronic accumulation. Worse in damp weather, mornings (phlegm pooling overnight), after heavy or greasy meals.
> warmth, movement, dry weather
< damp, cold, dairy/sugar, sedentary periods

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Heavy, achy limbs; generalized heaviness and lethargy in the muscles; possible joint stiffness with damp; puffy, edema-like fullness.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Profound mental fog, subjective "heaviness" of thought, difficulty concentrating, and complacency. The accumulation of Dampness blankets the cognitive faculties, leading to a state of mental dampening or inertia.

Biomedical Correlates
  • Chronic Bronchitis (COPD "blue bloater")
  • Bronchiectasis
  • Chronic post-nasal drip (UACS)
Confirming Lab Markers
  • High concentration of mucin on Sputum Analysis
  • Chest X-ray/CT showing increased bronchovascular markings, peribronchial cuffing, or hyperinflation
Herb Classes
  • Resolve lung damp/phlegm (warming)

Chinese herbs:

  • Pinellia (Ban xia)
  • Tangerine peel (Chen pi)
  • Mustard seed (Bai jie zi)
  • Perilla seed (Su zi)
  • Radish seed (Lai fu zi)

Western herbs:

  • Horehound (Marrubium vulgare)
  • Hyssop (Hyssopus officinalis)
  • Thyme (Thymus vulgaris)
  • Sage (Salvia officinalis)

  • Minerals/Metals: Cr (if insulin-resistance-linked), Ca
