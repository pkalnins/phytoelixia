Lung-Respiratory System · Patterns

External · Acute Dryness

Yin Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Fluid (Yin Axis): Acute external mucosal desiccation.

Pathophysiology
  • Acute environmental evaporation of airway surface liquid + sudden tight junction disruption
  • Acute upregulation of TRPV1/TRPA1 airway nociceptors due to environmental irritants
Gene / Signaling Pathways

Claudins/Occludins (tight junction breakdown), transient receptor potential channels (TRPV1/TRPA1 up-regulation causing the dry tickle), and acute mucin depletion.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Sudden onset dry cough, parched nose/lips, scratchy throat, dry skin, acute aversion to wind. Normal or slightly red tongue with a dry, rough, moisture-depleted coating; Floating-Thin (Fu Xi) pulse.

Tongue

Normal or slightly red body color, but the surface coating is noticeably dry, rough, and parched, lacking normal salivation.

Pulse

Floating-Thin (Fu Xi) or Floating-Rapid (Fu Shuo), sitting superficially on the vessel wall but feeling narrow in diameter.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Sudden onset with dry environmental exposure (wildfire smoke, dry season, air travel). Worse in dry climates, autumn.
> humidification, hydration
< dry wind, low humidity, smoke

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Dry, tight sensations in the throat and chest musculature; occasional dry-cough-induced rib/intercostal strain; dry skin and scalp tension.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Restlessness and irritability from the acute discomfort of dryness — a felt sense of being parched and depleted, short temper driven by the sheer physical irritation of a dry, tickling throat and cough.

Biomedical Correlates
  • Acute tracheobronchitis triggered by low-humidity environments
  • Dry-air induced airway hyperresponsiveness
  • Acute irritation from smoke inhalation (wildfires, industrial pollutants)
  • Early-stage viral pharyngitis/laryngitis before exudate forms
Confirming Lab Markers
  • Sputum Cytology showing sloughed epithelial cells without purulent inflammatory cells
  • Transiently elevated Serum Osmolality
