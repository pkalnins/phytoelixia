Lung-Respiratory System · Patterns

Internal · Lower Respiratory · Heat/Fire

Yang Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Chronic/Decongested inflammatory cascade (Heat Excess, Lower focus).

Pathophysiology
  • Massive neutrophilic infiltration, oxidative burst
  • Dense purulent exudate
  • Severe tissue remodeling/destruction
Gene / Signaling Pathways

Hyper-activated NF-κB, extreme up-regulation of Matrix Metalloproteinases (MMP−9 causing tissue breakdown), high IL−8 (neutrophil chemoattractant), and NOS2.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

A loud, barking, or painful cough with copious, thick, yellow-green, or foul-smelling purulent sputum (which may contain streaks of fresh blood). High fever, rapid labored breathing or wheezing (Chuan), flaring of the nostrils, chest pain that worsens with coughing, and intense thirst for cold drinks.

Tongue

Red body with a thick, yellow, greasy, or sticky coating.

Pulse

Slippery (Hua) and Rapid (Shuo), rolling quickly, forcefully, and fluidly.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Rapid, severe progression over 24-72 hours. Worse at night/lying flat (orthopnea).
> sitting upright, warmth, productive expectoration
< exertion, cold drinks (craved despite worsening damp)

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Chest wall pain worsened by coughing; intercostal muscle strain from forceful, barking cough; rib tenderness from sustained coughing fits.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

High anxiety, extreme agitation, panic or terror induced by air hunger (dyspnea), and delirium if the heat is severe enough to disturb the Shen (spirit).

Biomedical Correlates
  • Lobar or lobular bacterial pneumonia
  • Acute lung abscess
  • Severe acute exacerbation of chronic bronchitis (AECB)
Confirming Lab Markers
  • CBC with Differential: Marked leukocytosis with a left shift (high bands/immature neutrophils).
  • Procalcitonin (PCT): Elevated levels, strongly confirming a localized or systemic bacterial infection rather than viral or inflammatory origins.
  • Sputum Culture & Gram Stain: Positive for specific bacterial pathogens (e.g., Streptococcus pneumoniae, Haemophilus influenzae, Pseudomonas) along with abundant sheets of polymorphonuclear neutrophils (PMNs).
  • Imaging: Chest X-ray revealing dense lobar consolidation, air bronchograms, or fluid-filled cavitary lesions.
