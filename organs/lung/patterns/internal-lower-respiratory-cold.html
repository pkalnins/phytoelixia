Lung-Respiratory System · Patterns

Internal · Lower Respiratory · Cold

Yang Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Two aspects to Lung Yang:

  • Thyroid hormones (Lung Yang):
  • Adrenal androgens (Kidney Yang): DHEA/testosterone

Both are important to maintain overall lung metabolism

Pathophysiology
  • Mucociliary Arrest: Localized ATP deficit reduces bronchial ciliary beat frequency (CBF)
  • Aqueous Overflow: Downregulated T3​ suppresses epithelial sodium transport and aquaporins, triggering interstitial fluid pooling (alveolar edema)
  • Immune Senescence: Metabolic exhaustion in alveolar macrophages stunts phagocytosis
  • Surfactant Failure: ATP exhaustion in Type II pneumocytes compromises alveolar surface tension, leading to micro-atelectasis
Gene / Signaling Pathways

Downregulated: PPARGC1A (PGC-1alpha, mitochondrial biogenesis), UCP2 (uncoupling protein 2, thermogenesis), ATP5F1A (ATP synthase), AQP1 & AQP5 (aquaporin channels), THRA & THRB (thyroid receptors), AR (androgen receptor), PRKAA1 (AMPK, metabolic sensor).

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Chronic cough with profuse, clear, watery, or frothy white sputum. Shortness of breath (dyspnea) made worse by cold air or inactivity. Deep-seated chill in the upper back and chest. Cold extremities, bright pale/dusky complexion, lack of thirst, and spontaneous sweating under minimal exertion.

Tongue

Pale, swollen, and flabby tongue body with scalloped/toothmarked margins. Overly wet, moist, or slippery white coating.

Pulse

Deep (Chen), Weak/Thready (Ru), and Slow (Chi) or Slippery (Hua).

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Chronic, insidious onset. Worse in winter, cold/damp climates, and mornings before the body "warms up."
> warmth, rest, gentle movement
< cold, overexertion, sleep deprivation

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Generalized heaviness and weakness in the limbs, cold extremities with muscle stiffness, slowed reflexes, low back/knee weakness (Kidney-Yang territory).

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Low drive and motivation mirroring the metabolic slowing beneath it — mental heaviness, torpor, apathy, and cold-intolerance bleeding into mood. A sense of being "banked low": reluctance to initiate, diminished will-forces, reduced enthusiasm even for previously engaging tasks.

Biomedical Correlates
  • Hypothyroidism-induced respiratory dysfunction (subclinical interstitial pulmonary edema, reduced surfactant)
  • Stable Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) with mitochondrial decay
  • Senile emphysema with adrenal/androgenic decline (DHEA collapse)
  • Chronic wet asthma
Confirming Lab Markers
  • TSH elevated with free T3/T4 low-normal or low; possibly elevated reverse T3.
  • DHEA-S low; morning cortisol low-normal.
  • Consider ferritin if fatigue is prominent.
