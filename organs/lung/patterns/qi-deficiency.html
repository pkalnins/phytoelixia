Lung-Respiratory System · Patterns

Qi Deficiency

Qi Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis
  • Autonomic (Qi Axis): Sympathetic Nervous System (SNS) Hypoactivity leaving flaccid vagal tone dominant.
Pathophysiology
  • Suppressed mitochondrial biogenesis, downregulated ATP synthesis
  • Impaired innate mucosal immunity (Wei Qi barrier dysfunction)
Gene / Signaling Pathways

AMPK/mTOR, PGC-1α (mitochondrial biogenesis), Nrf2 (cellular resilience), and down-regulated IFN−γ.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Shortness of breath on mild exertion, soft/weak voice, spontaneous daytime sweating, susceptibility to frequent infections.

Tongue
  • Pale body
  • Often swollen
  • Teeth marks (indentations) on margins
  • Thin, moist, white coating.
Pulse

Empty (Xu) or Weak (Ruo), notably soft and lacking structural force, particularly in the right Cun (front/pulmonary) position.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Gradual onset, worsens with exertion and as the day progresses. Lung time 3-5 AM — early waking, fatigue on rising.
> rest, gentle breathing exercises
< overexertion, prolonged talking/singing, cold/damp

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Generalized fatigue-related muscle weakness, poor postural tone, shallow/weak diaphragmatic movement, slouched posture from low Qi.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Grief, sadness, or a lingering sense of melancholy that saps physical vitality. Individuals may display an lack of motivation, situational anxiety regarding their low energy, or withdrawal from social interactions due to physical vocal and respiratory fatigue.

Biomedical Correlates
  • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
  • Early-stage congestive heart failure (where pulmonary circulation lacks force)
  • Chronic fatigue syndrome (ME/CFS)
  • Recovering stages of acute viral bronchitis/pneumonia
  • Allergic rhinitis
Confirming Lab Markers
  • Pulmonary Function Tests (PFTs): Reduced FEV1​ (Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 second) and FVC (Forced Vital Capacity) indicating diminished lung volume or airflow restriction.
  • Immunological Profiles: Low total serum IgG or specific IgG subclasses, pointing to an impaired immune defense.
  • Metabolic Markers: Lowered morning cortisol or sub-optimal thyroid panels (Free T3​/T4​) that mimic low systemic energetic drive.
