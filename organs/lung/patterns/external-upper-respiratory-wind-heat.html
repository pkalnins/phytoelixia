Lung-Respiratory System · Patterns

External · Upper Respiratory · Wind-Heat

Yang Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Acute hyper-inflammatory vasodilation (Heat Excess, Upper focus).

Pathophysiology
  • Acute exudative inflammation
  • Rapid capillary vasodilation
  • Cellular infiltration
  • Pyrogenic cytokine cascades.
Gene / Signaling Pathways

NF-κB activation, up-regulated IL−1β, IL−6, TNF−α, and iNOS (causing massive localized nitric oxide/vasodilation/redness).

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Acute, forceful cough with thick, yellow or slightly turbid sputum. Fever that outweighs any mild aversion to wind/cold, a prominent sore, swollen, red throat, nasal congestion with yellow, thick discharge, thirst, and a sweating response that does not resolve the fever.

Tongue

Red tip or edges with a thin, dry, yellow coating.

Pulse

Floating (Fu) and Rapid (Shuo), sitting superficially and beating quickly.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Rapid onset (hours), typically peaking within 1-3 days. Worse afternoon/evening as fever rises.
> cooling, rest, fluids
< wind exposure, overheating, exertion

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Sore, swollen throat with pain on swallowing; tender submandibular/cervical lymph nodes; frontal or occipital headache from fever; upper trapezius and cervical tension accompanying the febrile response.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Restlessness, mild mental confusion or cloudiness due to pyrexia (fever), and acute irritability driven by throat pain and systemic heat.

Biomedical Correlates
  • Acute exudative tonsillitis
  • Streptococcal pharyngitis
  • Early acute bacterial bronchitis
  • Influenza
Confirming Lab Markers
  • CBC with Differential: Leukocytosis with a neutrophil shift (neutrophilia) if a secondary bacterial infection is taking hold.
  • Rapid Strep Test / Throat Culture: Positive for Group A Streptococcus or other bacterial pathogens.
  • Inflammatory Biomarkers: Elevated highly-sensitive C-Reactive Protein (hs-CRP).
Herb Classes
  • Cooling diaphoretics
  • Consider: demulcents, address Kidney Qi (Kidney "drainage"/diuresis)
