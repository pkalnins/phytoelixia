Liver-Biliary System · Patterns

Liver Fire Blazing

Yang Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Acute hyperinflammatory, hyperadrenergic surge concentrated in the hepatobiliary and upper-body vasculature.

Pathophysiology

Acute hepatic and biliary inflammatory cascade with elevated portal inflammatory tone; hyperadrenergic/hypercortisolemic activation driving vasodilation in the head and upper body; disrupted bile flow with cholestatic irritation.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

NF-κB activation, elevated hepatic IL-6/TNF-α, COX-2 upregulation, elevated circulating catecholamines and cortisol, transient endothelial nitric oxide surge in cephalic vasculature.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Sudden, severe, throbbing headache (temporal or vertex), red face and eyes, bitter taste in the mouth, marked thirst, constipation, dark scanty urine, sudden loud tinnitus, insomnia, explosive irritability or rage, and in severe cases nosebleed or hematemesis.

Tongue

Red body, especially at the sides, with a thick, dry, yellow coating.

Pulse

Wiry (Xian) and Rapid (Shuo), forceful and taut, most pronounced at the left Guan (middle/Liver) position.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Sudden onset, often precipitated by anger, frustration, or alcohol. Worse afternoon/evening.
> cooling, rest, emotional release
< anger, alcohol, greasy or spicy food, heat exposure

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Severe head, neck, and shoulder tension; jaw clenching; possible acute muscle spasm accompanying the headache.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Explosive anger, rage, and acute agitation — the classic TCM presentation of the Hun (Ethereal Soul) driven into overt excess; poor impulse control while the fire is active.

Biomedical Correlates

Acute hepatitis flare, acute cholecystitis, hypertensive urgency/crisis, acute angle-closure glaucoma, thyrotoxic crisis, acute manic or agitated psychiatric presentations.

Confirming Lab Markers

Liver Function Panel: Elevated AST/ALT, elevated GGT.

Inflammatory Markers: Elevated hs-CRP, leukocytosis.

Endocrine: Elevated morning cortisol, elevated urinary catecholamines; TSH suppressed with elevated free T4 if thyrotoxic.
