Liver-Biliary System · Patterns

Liver Yang Rising

Yang Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Sympathetic/vascular hyperactivity at the "branch," rooted in a Yin-deficient "root" (Liver/Kidney Yin deficiency failing to anchor Yang) — a deficiency-below, excess-above presentation categorized here by its presenting Yang-excess picture rather than its Yin-deficient origin.

Pathophysiology

Chronic sympathetic overactivity and vasoconstriction with reduced baroreceptor sensitivity, developing against a background of declining gonadal/adrenal Yin reserve (estrogen or androgen decline); endothelial dysfunction and RAAS activation sustain the elevated vascular tone over time.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Elevated angiotensin II signaling, reduced eNOS activity, elevated plasma renin/aldosterone in a subset, declining estrogen/testosterone receptor activity, elevated sympathetic outflow (reduced heart-rate variability).

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Dizziness and a sensation of the head being "too full," tinnitus, distending headache concentrated at the vertex and temples, flushed face when angry, dry mouth, irritability, insomnia — with underlying weakness or soreness in the low back and knees betraying the Yin-deficient root beneath the excess presentation.

Tongue

Red body, may be thinner or drier than in Liver Fire Blazing, with a thin yellow or patchy coating rather than a thick one.

Pulse

Wiry (Xian), but often thinner or weaker at the Chi (deep/Kidney) position than at the Guan (Liver) position — the pulse signature of excess above and deficiency below.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Fluctuating, worse with stress or emotional upset, often worse in spring (the Liver's associated season). Improves with rest and quiet.
> rest, quiet, emotional calm
< stress, sleep deprivation, overwork

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Neck and shoulder stiffness, tension headache radiating into the temples, occasional fine tremor of the hands.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Irritability, impatience, a headstrong or driven quality, difficulty relaxing even when physically at rest — restlessness with an exhausted undercurrent rather than the explosive rage of Liver Fire Blazing.

Biomedical Correlates

Essential hypertension (this pattern is TCM's classic correlate for it), menopausal hot flashes with concurrent hypertension, vertigo, early cerebrovascular risk/TIA.

Confirming Lab Markers

Cardiovascular: Elevated systolic and diastolic blood pressure; reduced heart-rate variability.

Endocrine: Low-normal or declining estradiol/testosterone for age; elevated homocysteine in some presentations.

Vascular Imaging: Elevated carotid intima-media thickness where cerebrovascular risk is a concern.
