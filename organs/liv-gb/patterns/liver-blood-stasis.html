Liver-Biliary System · Patterns

Liver Blood Stasis

Blood Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Circulatory/Nutritive (Blood Axis): Congestive, fibrotic remodeling of hepatic microcirculation with impaired chemical exchange and venous outflow.

Pathophysiology

Hepatic stellate cell activation and progressive collagen deposition (fibrosis), portal venous congestion, and impaired hepatic microcirculation; in the reproductive territory, pelvic venous stasis and endometrial tissue congestion follow the same stagnation logic.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

TGF-β-driven stellate cell activation, elevated collagen I/III deposition, elevated tissue inhibitor of metalloproteinases (TIMP-1) relative to matrix metalloproteinases, portal hypertension-associated VEGF upregulation.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Fixed, stabbing hypochondriac pain that resists movement, a palpable mass or firmness on abdominal exam, dark or dusky facial complexion, visible varicosities, dysmenorrhea with dark clotted menstrual blood.

Tongue

Dark purple or dusky body, possibly with visible stasis spots or a sublingual venous engorgement.

Pulse

Choppy (Se) or Wiry (Xian), feeling rough and uneven rather than smoothly flowing.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Chronic, slowly progressive, worsening over months to years if unaddressed. Pain worse at night and with pressure; fixed in location rather than migratory.
> warmth, gentle movement, massage
< prolonged immobility, cold, emotional suppression

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Fixed stabbing rib-side pain worse with pressure, varicosities in the lower limbs, pelvic congestion with dull aching worse premenstrually.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Suppressed rage, resentment, and "stuck" emotional patterns held over long periods rather than expressed — the somatic residue of Liver Qi Stagnation left unresolved long enough to condense into the Blood level.

Biomedical Correlates

Hepatic fibrosis and early cirrhosis, portal hypertension, elevated hepatocellular carcinoma risk in advanced fibrosis, endometriosis, uterine fibroids, pelvic congestion syndrome.

Confirming Lab Markers

Hepatic: Elevated AST/ALT with AST often exceeding ALT in advanced fibrosis; low platelet count (portal hypertension/hypersplenism); elevated INR reflecting reduced synthetic function.

Imaging: Elastography (FibroScan) showing elevated liver stiffness; ultrasound or MRI showing nodularity, splenomegaly, or portal vein dilation.

Gynecologic: Pelvic ultrasound or MRI showing fibroids, endometriomas, or venous congestion where relevant.
