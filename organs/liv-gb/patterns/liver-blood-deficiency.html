Liver-Biliary System · Patterns

Liver Blood Deficiency

Blood Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Circulatory/Nutritive (Blood Axis): Systemic nutritive/hematologic depletion, most apparent in the tissues the Liver is said to specifically nourish — the eyes, tendons, and nails.

Pathophysiology

Reduced circulating nutritive capacity (iron, B12, folate, or protein-calorie insufficiency) leading to tissue hypoperfusion and undernourishment of tendon, ocular, and dermal tissue; reduced hepatic glycogen and micronutrient reserve compounds fatigue and muscle cramping.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Reduced erythropoietin responsiveness in iron-restricted states, elevated hepcidin in inflammation-associated anemia, reduced retinol-binding protein transport (Vitamin A-dependent ocular surface health), reduced muscle protein synthesis signaling (mTOR pathway) under chronic nutritive insufficiency.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Dizziness, blurred vision, floaters, dry or gritty eyes, pale nail beds, numbness or tingling in the extremities, scanty pale menses or amenorrhea, muscle cramps (especially nocturnal calf cramps), fine tremor, pale lips.

Tongue

Pale body, thin, may appear slightly dry.

Pulse

Thready (Xi) or Choppy (Se), fine and difficult to locate, especially at the left Guan position.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Gradual onset, worse with fatigue, overwork, or after menstruation. Worse premenstrually and postpartum.
> rest, nourishing food, adequate sleep
< overexertion, excessive menstrual loss, chronic dieting/undernutrition

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Nocturnal muscle cramps (especially calves), tendon stiffness, brittle nails, numbness or tingling in the limbs, generalized weakness.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Anxiety, poor sleep with vivid or unsettled dreaming, indecisiveness, timidity, easy startling — the Hun described classically as having "nowhere to rest" when Liver Blood is insufficient to house it at night.

Biomedical Correlates

Iron-deficiency anemia, Vitamin A deficiency (night blindness, dry eyes), amenorrhea or oligomenorrhea, peripheral neuropathy, age- or nutrition-related sarcopenia.

Confirming Lab Markers

Hematologic: Low hemoglobin/hematocrit, low ferritin, low serum iron with elevated total iron-binding capacity.

Nutritional: Low B12/folate; low serum retinol (Vitamin A) if ocular symptoms are prominent.

Endocrine: Low-normal estradiol in amenorrhea/oligomenorrhea presentations.
