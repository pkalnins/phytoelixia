Spleen-GI System · Patterns

Stomach Qi Rebellion

Qi Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Autonomic (Qi Axis): Disrupted gastric motility with retrograde (upward) rather than descending functional tone.

Pathophysiology

Impaired antral motility and lower esophageal sphincter tone, vagal dysregulation, and delayed gastric emptying with retrograde reflux of gastric contents.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Altered motilin and ghrelin signaling, reduced vagal efferent tone, elevated 5-HT3 receptor activity (nausea/emesis pathway), reduced interstitial cells of Cajal pacemaker activity in some chronic presentations.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Nausea, belching, hiccups, acid reflux, a sensation of fullness or blockage in the epigastrium, occasional vomiting — Stomach Qi that should descend instead rises.

Tongue

Normal or slightly red body, thin white or yellow coating depending on associated heat.

Pulse

Wiry (Xian) or Slippery (Hua) at the right Guan (Stomach) position.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Episodic, often triggered by stress, overeating, or eating too quickly. Worse immediately after meals.
> small frequent meals, slow eating, calm environment
< stress while eating, overeating, lying down soon after meals

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Epigastric and lower-chest tightness, diaphragmatic tension, occasional referred discomfort between the shoulder blades with reflux.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Anxiety concentrated around eating and digestion, a sense of things "not settling," mild food-related hypervigilance in chronic presentations.

Biomedical Correlates

GERD, functional dyspepsia, cyclic vomiting syndrome, chemotherapy- or motion-induced nausea, rumination syndrome.

Confirming Lab Markers

GI Workup: Upper endoscopy typically normal or shows only mild esophagitis; gastric emptying study may show delayed or occasionally accelerated transit.

Autonomic: Reduced heart-rate variability in stress-triggered presentations.
