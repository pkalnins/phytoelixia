Spleen-GI System · Patterns

Large Intestine Damp-Heat

Yin Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Fluid (Yin Axis): Acute inflammatory fluid/mucosal burden in the colon, with infectious or dysbiotic drivers.

Pathophysiology

Colonic mucosal inflammation, dysbiosis with pathogenic bacterial overgrowth, increased intestinal permeability, and cytokine-driven secretory diarrhea.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Elevated colonic NF-κB and TNF-α signaling, elevated calprotectin (neutrophilic mucosal inflammation), disrupted tight-junction protein expression (claudins/occludins), pathogen-associated TLR4 activation.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Acute diarrhea with foul odor, burning sensation at the anus, abdominal cramping with urgency, possible blood or mucus in the stool, thirst, dark scanty urine.

Tongue

Red body with a thick, greasy, yellow coating.

Pulse

Slippery (Hua) and Rapid (Shuo), rolling quickly and forcefully.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Acute onset, often following contaminated food/water exposure or during an inflammatory bowel disease flare. Worse in humid weather and summer.
> hydration, rest, bland diet during acute phase
< contaminated food/water, alcohol, spicy/greasy food, humidity

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Abdominal cramping with urgency, perianal irritation/burning, generalized malaise during acute flares.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Anxiety and urgency-related hypervigilance around bathroom access, irritability compounded by physical discomfort and disrupted sleep during flares.

Biomedical Correlates

Infectious colitis (bacterial dysentery, C. difficile), inflammatory bowel disease flare (ulcerative colitis in particular), acute gastroenteritis.

Confirming Lab Markers

Stool Studies: Elevated fecal calprotectin; stool culture/PCR positive for pathogenic organisms where infectious.

Inflammatory Markers: Elevated CRP/ESR; leukocytosis in acute infectious presentations.

Herb Classes

Clear damp-heat, stop dysentery
