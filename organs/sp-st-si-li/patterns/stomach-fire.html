Spleen-GI System · Patterns

Stomach Fire

Yang Excess
Primary Biological Axis

Metabolic/Immune (Yang Axis): Acute hyperacidic, hyperinflammatory activity concentrated in the gastric mucosa and oral cavity.

Pathophysiology

Gastric mucosal hyperacidity with elevated acid secretion, frequently H. pylori-associated inflammatory injury, and secondary oral-cavity inflammation from ascending heat.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Elevated gastrin signaling, H+/K+-ATPase (proton pump) hyperactivity, NF-κB-driven gastric mucosal inflammation, elevated IL-8 in H. pylori-associated presentations.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Rapid, excessive hunger with quick digestion, burning epigastric pain, bad breath, bleeding or swollen gums, marked thirst for cold drinks, constipation.

Tongue

Red body, especially at the center, with a thick, dry, yellow coating.

Pulse

Slippery (Hua) and Rapid (Shuo), forceful at the right Guan (Stomach) position.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Acute or acute-on-chronic, worse after spicy, fried, or alcohol-heavy meals. Worse afternoon and evening.
> cooling foods, rest, hydration
< spicy/fried food, alcohol, overeating, heat exposure

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Epigastric burning worse with pressure, jaw tension from gum inflammation, occasional referred discomfort between the shoulder blades.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Irritability and impatience, often with a driven, appetite-forward restlessness — the excess heat manifesting as urgency rather than the explosive rage seen in Liver Fire.

Biomedical Correlates

Peptic ulcer disease (H. pylori-associated), erosive gastritis, GERD with esophagitis, gingivitis and periodontal disease.

Confirming Lab Markers

GI Workup: H. pylori stool antigen or urea breath test positive; endoscopy showing mucosal erosion or ulceration.

Inflammatory Markers: Elevated hs-CRP in active inflammation.
