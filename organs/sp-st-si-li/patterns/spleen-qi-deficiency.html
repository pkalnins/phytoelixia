Spleen-GI System · Patterns

Spleen Qi Deficiency

Qi Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Autonomic (Qi Axis): Reduced functional/digestive drive across the Spleen-Stomach axis — the foundational deficiency pattern of this organ system.

Pathophysiology

Reduced digestive and absorptive capacity, impaired nutrient transport across the intestinal mucosa, and a chronic mitochondrial energy deficit secondary to sustained malabsorption.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Reduced AMPK/mTOR nutrient-sensing activity, down-regulated intestinal nutrient transporters (SGLT1, GLUT2), reduced mitochondrial biogenesis (PGC-1α) in enterocytes, blunted digestive enzyme gene expression.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Fatigue, poor appetite, loose stools, bloating and distension worse after eating, weak limbs, pale complexion, a tendency to bruise easily.

Tongue

Pale body, often swollen, with teeth-mark indentations on the margins; thin white coating.

Pulse

Empty (Xu) or Weak (Ruo), soft and lacking force, most notable at the right Guan (Spleen) position.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Gradual onset, worsens with exertion and as the day progresses. Worse after eating, better with rest.
> rest after meals, warm cooked food, moderate activity
< overexertion, worry/overthinking, irregular meals, cold/raw food

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Generalized fatigue-related muscle weakness, poor postural tone, heavy or weak limbs, easy bruising from poor Qi containment of Blood.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Worry and overthinking (the Spleen's classically associated emotion), a ruminative quality to mental activity, indecisiveness, and a tendency toward self-doubt that saps motivation rather than provoking anxiety outright.

Biomedical Correlates

Malabsorption syndromes, chronic fatigue with a prominent GI component, undifferentiated/mixed-type IBS, mild anemia of chronic disease, sarcopenia from poor nutrient absorption.

Confirming Lab Markers

Nutritional: Low-normal albumin/prealbumin; low ferritin; reduced fecal elastase if pancreatic exocrine function is also reduced.

Hematologic: Mild normocytic anemia consistent with chronic disease/malabsorption.
