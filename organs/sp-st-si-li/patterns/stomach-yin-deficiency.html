Spleen-GI System · Patterns

Stomach Yin Deficiency

Yin Deficiency
Primary Biological Axis

Fluid (Yin Axis): Chronic structural fluid depletion of the gastric mucosa, with reduced secretory reserve rather than active inflammation.

Pathophysiology

Reduced gastric mucosal secretory reserve, mucosal atrophy, and a thinned protective mucin layer, typically developing after prolonged heat-pattern illness, chronic stress, or repeated mucosal injury.

Gene / Signaling Pathways

Reduced gastric mucin (MUC5AC) expression, parietal cell atrophy with reduced intrinsic factor production in advanced cases, chronic low-grade IL-6 elevation without acute flare markers.

Key Symptoms & Clinical Findings

Hunger without desire to eat, dry mouth and throat, dry stools, mild epigastric discomfort or burning, and a general sense of digestive dryness.

Tongue

Red body, thin and dry, with little or no coating — the classic "mirror tongue" of Stomach Yin deficiency.

Pulse

Thready (Xi) and Rapid (Shuo), fine and fast at the right Guan (Stomach) position.

Temporal / Qualitative Patterns

Chronic, insidious onset, often following a prolonged febrile illness or chronic stress. Worse in the afternoon and in dry, hot weather.
> rest, moistening foods, cool quiet environments
< heat exposure, dry climates, overwork, insufficient sleep

Musculoskeletal / Pain Expression

Mild generalized dryness-related stiffness, low-grade fatigue without significant structural pain.

Psychological / Consciousness Expression

Restlessness, mild irritability, a subtle "running on empty" quality distinct from the heavier fatigue of Qi deficiency — the mind staying active while the body's own reserves are depleted.

Biomedical Correlates

Atrophic gastritis, Sjögren's-associated GI dryness, post-chemotherapy or post-radiation mucosal atrophy, chronic acid-suppressive therapy with associated mucosal changes.

Confirming Lab Markers

GI Workup: Endoscopy with biopsy showing mucosal atrophy where advanced; elevated gastrin in some atrophic presentations.

Autoimmune: Positive anti-parietal cell or anti-intrinsic factor antibodies if pernicious anemia is a consideration.
