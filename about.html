About This Project

The Salutogenic Medicine Project is a working clinical and teaching reference that maps Traditional Chinese Medicine's pattern differentiation onto biomedical physiology, Western herbalism, and anthroposophic medicine — organ by organ, pattern by pattern.

Its organizing question is salutogenesis: not just how disease arises and is treated, but how health itself arises and is actively maintained. Conventional biomedicine is built almost entirely around the opposite question, pathogenesis — the origins, pathways, and elimination of specific disease states. This project treats salutogenic medicine not as a replacement for that model, but as its missing half, and uses the classical Chinese Yang/Qi/Blood/Yin framework, read through Steiner's four-bodies physiology, as the vocabulary for describing it.

How it's organized. Foundations lays the shared philosophical and physiological groundwork once, read ahead of everything else. Glands and Organs each build out that framework for a specific part of the body — phenomenology, physiology, clinical patterns, and the biomedical conditions those patterns predominate in — and Framework holds the eight universal pattern types (Yang/Qi/Blood/Yin × Excess/Deficiency) that every organ and gland chapter is built from.

Where it stands. This is a living, actively-drafted document, not a finished text. The Lung module is the most complete, built from real clinical source material; the other organ modules are first-pass drafts pending clinical review, and several Foundations chapters and all seven glands are still placeholders. Treat everything here as a draft mentorship-group edition — a working reference for an active teaching group, not a publication.
